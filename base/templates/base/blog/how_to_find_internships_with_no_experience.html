The 5-step playbook to get internships with no experience

StepWhat to doOutcome
1) Define a lane Pick one target (marketing • finance • software • design • ops). Clear keywords and companies to chase.
2) Build tiny proof Ship a 1–2 day project aligned to the lane. A link that replaces “experience.”
3) Apply widely (smart) 50–150 targeted apps; prioritize rolling & SMBs. Interviews from volume + fit.
4) Ask for short trials Offer a 1–2 week trial or part-time help. Lowers risk for the company; faster yes.
5) Iterate weekly Improve project, resume keywords, and where you search. Compounding conversion gains.

Fast proof projects you can build this week

Pick one aligned to your lane. Done & linked beats perfect.

  • Marketing: 30-day content calendar + 6 demo posts for a local business; track clicks with UTM links.
  • Data: Public dataset → cleaned CSV → simple dashboard (Looker/Tableau) with a 1-page summary.
  • Software: Tiny web app (login + one feature) or a script that automates a boring task; include README + demo GIF.
  • Design/UX: Landing page redesign for a nonprofit; include mobile mocks and a small component library.
  • Ops/Finance: Expense tracker with monthly roll-up and insights (e.g., “cut cost X by Y%”).

Resume & ATS tips when you have no experience

  • Lead with proof: Projects, tools, and outcomes in the top third.
  • Mirror keywords: Use exact phrases from the posting (Excel, Python, Canva, CAD, CRM).
  • One page, clean layout: Standard section names; export as PDF.
  • Link out: Portfolio, GitHub, dashboard, or Google Drive doc with viewer access.

Common mistakes that block offers

  • Waiting for experience: Build tiny proof now; experience follows output.
  • Generic materials: Mirror each posting’s keywords; adjust your top third.
  • Low volume: Aim for steady, targeted volume all season.
  • No follow-up: Follow up once after 5–7 days with a small new insight.

FAQ

What if I’m a first-year student?

Target insight programs, campus roles, nonprofits, and local businesses. Your goal is a linkable proof project and a good reference.

How long should I spend on a proof project?

1–2 days to ship v1. Improve based on feedback. Keep scope small and value obvious.

How many applications should I send weekly?

10–20 targeted applications plus regular checks of your company list and alerts.
