How to Write Resume for Internship (Step-by-Step Guide for Students)

Written by Anna Blanchard
Updated: Oct 31, 2025

Writing a resume for an internship is stressful when you feel like you don’t have “real experience.” Most students apply to 50+ internships before they get a single reply. A lot of good candidates never hear back — not because they’re unqualified, but because their resume doesn’t highlight what matters.

This guide shows you exactly how to write resume for internship applications, even if you’re a first-year student with no formal job history.

1. Internship Resume Format (What Order to Use)

Use this structure. It’s clean, and it works for recruiters and applicant tracking systems (ATS):

  1. Contact info
  2. Short objective
  3. Education
  4. Experience
  5. Skills
  6. Achievements / leadership

1.1 Contact Header

At the very top, include:

  • Full name
  • Phone number
  • Professional email
  • City + country
  • LinkedIn profile
  • Portfolio / GitHub (if relevant)

Do not include age, photo, or marital status. Recruiters just want to reach you quickly.

1.2 Resume Objective

Your objective replaces “5 years of work experience.” Keep it to 2–3 lines. Say what you study, what you can do, and what you want.

Example (Marketing student):
“Business student with hands-on social media experience for a local café. Looking for a marketing internship where I can apply data-driven content strategy, paid social, and short-form video growth skills.”

This is perfect for anyone searching “internship resume with no experience,” because it shows what you can do now.

1.3 Education

For most internship applications, your education is the core of the resume.

  • School + degree
  • Graduation year (or “Expected June 2027”)
  • Relevant coursework
  • GPA (if strong)
  • Scholarships / honors

Example:
Copenhagen Business School
BSc International Business, Expected June 2027
Relevant coursework: Financial Accounting, Excel for Business Analytics, Marketing Strategy
Dean’s List 2024

If you’re googling “how to write resume for internship with no experience,” this is where you win. Your coursework is experience.

1.4 Experience

“Experience” does not have to mean “official full-time corporate job.” You can list:

  • Part-time work (retail, café, babysitting, etc.)
  • Projects and coursework
  • Student clubs
  • Volunteer work
  • Freelance work for a local business

The bullet format that works is: action verb + what you did + measurable result.

Bad: “Helped with social media.”
Good: “Created 12 Instagram/TikTok posts per week for a local café, helping increase weekend sales by ~18%.”

1.5 Skills (Keywords for ATS)

Split skills into two groups:

  • Technical / Tools: Excel, Python, PowerPoint, Canva, HubSpot, Figma, SQL
  • Soft / Business: Communication, research, presentation, attention to detail

Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) scan resumes for keywords. If the internship description says “Excel,” “Python,” or “market research,” put those exact words in your Skills section. Otherwise you can get filtered out even if you’re qualified.

1.6 Achievements / Leadership / Extracurriculars

This section quietly makes you stand out from everyone else using a generic template:

  • Treasurer, Finance Club — managed €5,000 budget
  • Top 10 / 120 teams in Deloitte Case Competition 2025
  • Volunteered at TechGirls coding camp, mentoring 12 girls in basic Python

This shows initiative. That’s what hiring managers actually look for.

2. Common Resume Mistakes (and How to Fix Them)

2.1 Filling with irrelevant info

If you’re applying for a finance internship, don’t spend five lines on being a camp counselor. You can mention it, but frame it in terms of responsibility, budgeting, safety, or leadership.

2.2 Sending the same resume everywhere

Recruiters can smell copy-paste. ATS can also reject resumes that don’t match role-specific keywords.

How AI helps: Internstart tailors your resume keywords to each internship (finance, marketing, data, etc.) and then applies on your behalf. You get relevance + volume without rewriting the same thing 200 times.

2.3 Submitting weird formats

Don’t upload .pages files, screenshots, or super-designed Canva resumes full of icons. Keep it a clean PDF with normal text and headings so ATS can read it.

2.4 Wasting the top of the page

The first third of the page should show why you’re useful immediately: objective, skills, education. Not hobbies.

3. Internship Resume Examples You Can Copy

Marketing Internship Resume Example

Objective:
“Business student with hands-on social media work for a local café. Looking for a marketing internship to apply content strategy, paid social, and short-form video growth skills.”

Strong bullet:
Ran TikTok and Instagram promotions (~€200/mo spend) and helped increase weekend sales by ~18%.

Skills: Canva, TikTok Ads Manager, Instagram Reels editing, copywriting, basic analytics

Computer Science / Software Internship Resume Example

Objective:
“First-year CS student with experience using Python to automate simple data tasks. Interested in a software internship with exposure to backend or data workflows.”

Strong bullet:
Built a Python script that scraped pricing data from 20+ e-commerce sites and exported it to Excel.

Skills: Python, GitHub, HTML/CSS, basic SQL, data cleaning in Pandas

Finance Internship Resume Example

Objective:
“Finance student with strong Excel and budgeting experience from student organizations. Seeking a summer finance internship to apply analytical and reporting skills.”

Strong bullet:
Managed a €5,000 student club budget and built monthly spend reports in Excel.

Skills: Excel (PivotTables, VLOOKUP), PowerPoint, market research, basic financial modeling

4. How to Stand Out vs Other Applicants

Everyone writes “hardworking” and “motivated.” That doesn’t stand out. What stands out is proof you can actually do useful things in the real world.

Marketing / Growth

What matters: TikTok Ads Manager, short-form video, Canva, content that converts.

How to write it:
“Created and A/B tested ad creatives for TikTok campaigns promoting local events, reaching ~15,000 views in 7 days.”

Finance / Business / Ops

What matters: Excel (PivotTables, VLOOKUP), PowerPoint, budget tracking.

How to write it:
“Built monthly cashflow and expense tracking spreadsheets in Excel for a student organization and presented insights to leadership.”

Data / Tech

What matters: Python automation, basic SQL, GitHub, data cleaning.

How to write it:
“Used Python to collect and clean 500+ product listings from public sites and stored the results in a searchable Excel sheet.”

Add bullets like these to your Skills and Experience sections. This is what gets you interviews.
