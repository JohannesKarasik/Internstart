Why your Cover Letter for Internship matters

Recruiters skim dozens of similar resumes. A concise cover letter shows communication skills, motivation, and specific fit for the team. It often decides who gets the interview.

The 5-part structure that works

PartWhat to writePro tip
HeaderName, contact, date, company, role.Match your resume header.
Hook1–2 lines tying your skills to their goals.Reference a product, campaign, or mission.
FitMirror 3–5 keywords from the posting.Use exact phrases (Excel, Python, CAD, SEO).
Proof1–2 mini results with numbers.“Built X → got Y result in Z time.”
Close1 line of enthusiasm + availability.“Available May–August.”

Copy-and-paste template

Dear Hiring Manager,

I’m a second-year Business student at Copenhagen Business School applying for the Marketing Intern role at BrightBean. I’m excited by your creator marketing program and can help with Excel reporting, social content, and customer research.

Recently, I planned a month of Reels/TikToks that reached ~42,000 views and grew sign-ups 19%. I also built an Airtable to track posts and UTM links so we knew what drove event RSVPs. These projects taught me to ship useful work quickly, communicate clearly, and ask better questions.

I’m available May–August and would love to support the marketing team.

Best regards,
Maya Nilsson
maya.nilsson@email.com · +1 (555) 123-4567 · linkedin.com/in/mayanilsson


Short examples you can model



Example — No Experience (Marketing Intern)


Dear Hiring Manager,

I’m a first-year Business student at CBS applying for the Marketing Intern role at BrightBean. I love how your TikTok spotlights student creators and I’d like to help scale that.

For my campus club, I scheduled a month of Reels/TikToks that reached ~42,000 views and grew sign-ups 19%. I also set up UTM tracking in Airtable to see which posts drove RSVPs.

I’m available June–August and can support content calendaring, short-form video, and basic analytics.

Best,
Maya Nilsson


Example — Software Engineering Intern


Dear Ms. López,

I’m a CS student (Python/JS) applying for the Software Engineering Intern role at Orbit. Your shift to edge-rendered dashboards is exciting; I’ve been prototyping similar charts.

Last semester I built a Flask API that aggregates canteen prices and a React UI to chart them. ~600 students used it in week one. I added tests and CI, which cut bugs 35% by mid-term.

I’m available May–August and happy to help with frontend components, data fetching, and bug triage.

Best regards,
Arun Patel

Common mistakes to avoid


    
  
  • Vague claims. Replace “hard-working team player” with a one-line proof and a number.
    • 
  
  • Same letter everywhere. Customize the hook and 3–5 keywords for each role.
    • 
  
  • Wall of text. Use short paragraphs; make skimming effortless.
    • 
  
  • No availability. Always state months (e.g., “May–August”).

FAQ


Do I need a Cover Letter for Internship if the job doesn’t ask?


If there’s a field to add one, do it. On competitive, rolling internships, a short letter improves your odds.



How do I address it without a name?


Use “Dear Hiring Manager” and reference the specific team or program in your opening line.



What if I’m switching fields?


Lead with transferable skills and a tiny proof project (dashboard, script, or design). One tangible result beats adjectives.
