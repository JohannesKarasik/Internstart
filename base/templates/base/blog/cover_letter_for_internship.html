Why your cover letter for internship matters

When recruiters see dozens of similar resumes, a tight cover letter is the tie-breaker. It shows communication skills, motivation, and fit for the specific team. The trick is to be concrete and brief—show impact, not adjectives.

The 5-part structure that wins interviews

PartWhat to writePro tip
Header Name, contact, date, company, role. Match your resume header; link LinkedIn/portfolio.
Hook 1–2 lines tying your skills to the team’s goals. Reference a product, campaign, or mission they care about.
Fit Mirror 3–5 keywords from the posting. Use exact phrases (Excel, Python, social content, CAD).
Proof 1–2 STAR-style mini stories with numbers. “Built X that led to Y result in Z time.”
Close 1 line of enthusiasm + availability. “I’m available May–August; I’d love to help with ___.”

Copy-and-paste template (fill in under 5 minutes)

Dear Hiring Manager,


I’m a second-year Business student at Copenhagen Business School applying for the Marketing Intern role at BrightBean. I’m excited by your creator marketing program and believe I can help with Excel reporting, social content, and customer research.

Recently, I planned a month of Reels/TikToks that reached ~42,000 views and grew sign-ups 19%. In another project, I built a simple Airtable to track posts and UTM links so we knew what drove event RSVPs. These experiences taught me to ship useful work quickly, communicate clearly, and ask better questions.

I’m available May–August and eager to contribute to the marketing team. I’d welcome the chance to learn more about your goals and how I can help.

Best regards,
Maya Nilsson
+1 (555) 123-4567 · maya.nilsson@email.com · linkedin.com/in/mayanilsson


Examples you can model




Example 1 — No experience (Marketing Intern)


Dear Hiring Manager,


I’m a first-year Business student at CBS applying for the Marketing Intern role at BrightBean. I love how your TikTok spotlights student creators; I’d like to help scale that.

For my campus club, I planned a month of Reels/TikToks that reached ~42,000 views and grew sign-ups 19%. I also built a simple Airtable to track posts and UTM links, so we knew what drove event RSVPs.

I’m available June–August and would love to support content calendaring, short-form video, and basic analytics.

Best,
Maya Nilsson
LinkedIn · Portfolio


Example 2 — Software Engineering Intern


Dear Ms. López,


I’m a CS student (Python/JS) applying for the Software Engineering Intern role at Orbit. Your recent move to edge-rendered dashboards is exciting; I’ve been prototyping similar charts.

Last semester, I built a Flask API that aggregates school canteen prices and displays charts in a responsive React app. ~600 students used it in the first week. I also wrote tests and CI, which cut bugs 35% by mid-term.

I’m available May–August and happy to help with frontend components, data fetching, and bug triage.

Best regards,
Arun Patel
GitHub · LinkedIn

ATS keywords & formatting tips


    
  
  • Mirror exact keywords from the posting (e.g., “PivotTables,” “customer interviews,” “Django,” “SolidWorks”).
    • 
  
  • Keep to one page, 200–350 words. Use normal fonts and standard section names.

  • Save as PDF named “Firstname-Lastname-Internship-Cover-Letter.pdf”.

  • If the platform has a text box, paste the text and attach the PDF as well.

Common mistakes to avoid


    
  
  • Vague claims: Replace “hard-working team player” with a 1-line proof and a number.
    • 
  
  • Copy-pasting the same letter: Customize the hook and 3–5 keywords for each role.
    • 
  
  • Wall of text: Use short paragraphs. Make skimming effortless.
    • 
  
  • No availability: Always state your months (e.g., “May–August”).

FAQ




Do I need a cover letter if the job doesn’t ask?


If there’s a field to add one, do it. For competitive internships, a short, tailored letter improves your odds.




How do I address it without a name?


Use “Dear Hiring Manager” and reference the specific team or program in your opening line.




What if I’m switching fields?


Lead with transferable skills and a quick proof (e.g., a small project, certificate, or volunteer work) that shows you can contribute immediately.
