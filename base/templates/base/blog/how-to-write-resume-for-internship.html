1. Internship Resume Format: How to Structure It

Use this order. This is the clean, proven internship resume format that recruiters and applicant tracking systems (ATS) can read fast.

1.1 Contact Header

At the very top, include:

  • Full name
  • Phone number
  • Professional email
  • City + country
  • LinkedIn profile
  • Portfolio / GitHub (if relevant)

Do not include age, photo, or marital status. Recruiters just want to reach you quickly.

1.2 Resume Objective

Your objective replaces “5 years of work experience.” Keep it to 2–3 lines. Say what you study, what you can do, and what you want.

Example (Marketing student)

Objective: Business student with hands-on social media experience for a local café. Looking for a marketing internship where I can apply data-driven content strategy, paid social, and short-form video growth skills.

This is perfect for anyone searching “internship resume with no experience” because it proves you have value today.

1.3 Education

For most internship applications, your education is the core of the resume.

  • School + degree
  • Graduation year (or “Expected June 2027”)
  • Relevant coursework
  • GPA (if strong)
  • Scholarships / honors
Example

Copenhagen Business School
BSc International Business, Expected June 2027
Relevant coursework: Financial Accounting, Excel for Business Analytics, Marketing Strategy
Dean’s List 2024

If you’re googling “how to write resume for internship with no experience,” this is where you win. Your coursework is experience.

1.4 Experience

“Experience” does not have to mean “official full-time corporate job.”

  • Part-time work (retail, café, babysitting, etc.)
  • Projects and coursework
  • Student clubs
  • Volunteer roles
  • Freelance work for a local business

Bullet format should be: action verb + what you did + measurable result.

Bad vs Good

Bad: Helped with social media.

Good: Created 12 Instagram/TikTok posts per week for a local café, helping increase weekend sales by ~18%.

1.5 Skills (Keywords for ATS)

Split skills into two groups:

  • Technical / Tools: Excel, Python, PowerPoint, Canva, HubSpot, Figma, SQL
  • Soft / Business: Communication, research, presentation, attention to detail

Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) scan resumes for keywords. If the internship description says “Excel,” “Python,” or “market research,” put those exact words in your Skills section or you might get filtered out — even if you’re actually qualified.

1.6 Achievements / Leadership / Extracurriculars

This section quietly makes you stand out from everyone else applying with a generic template resume.

  • Treasurer, Finance Club — managed €5,000 budget
  • Placed Top 10/120 teams in Deloitte Case Competition 2025
  • Volunteered at TechGirls coding camp, mentoring 12 girls in basic Python

This is what hiring managers really want in an internship resume format: proof that you take initiative.

2. Common Resume Mistakes (and How to Fix Them)

2.1 Filling with irrelevant info

If you’re applying for a finance internship, don’t spend five lines on being a camp counselor. You can mention it, but frame it in terms of responsibility, budgeting, safety, or leadership.

2.2 Sending the same resume everywhere

Recruiters can smell copy-paste. Also, ATS often auto-rejects resumes that don’t match role-specific keywords.

2.3 Submitting weird formats

Don’t upload .pages, screenshots, or super-designed Canva resumes with icons everywhere. Keep it a clean PDF, normal text, normal headings. ATS needs to read it.

2.4 Wasting the top part of the page

The first third of the page should communicate why you’re useful immediately: objective, skills, education. Not hobbies.

3. Internship Resume Examples (Marketing / Tech / Finance)

Use these internship resume examples as a model. Keep yours to one page. These are realistic for students with little or no experience.

Marketing Internship Resume Example

Objective: Business student with hands-on social media work for a local café. Looking for a marketing internship to apply content strategy, paid social, and short-form video growth skills.

Experience bullet:
Ran TikTok and Instagram promotions (~€200/mo spend) and helped increase weekend sales by ~18%.

Skills: Canva, TikTok Ads Manager, Instagram Reels editing, copywriting, basic analytics

Computer Science / Software Internship Resume Example

Objective: First-year CS student with experience using Python to automate simple data tasks. Interested in a software internship with exposure to backend or data workflows.

Experience bullet:
Built a Python script that scraped pricing data from 20+ e-commerce sites and exported it to Excel.

Skills: Python, GitHub, HTML/CSS, basic SQL, data cleaning in Pandas

Finance Internship Resume Example

Objective: Finance student with strong Excel and budgeting experience from student organizations. Seeking a summer finance internship to apply analytical and reporting skills.

Experience bullet:
Managed a €5,000 student club budget and built monthly spend reports in Excel.

Skills: Excel (PivotTables, VLOOKUP), PowerPoint, market research, basic financial modeling

4. How to Stand Out vs Other Applicants

Everyone says “hardworking.” You will not stand out with that. You stand out by showing useful tools.

Marketing / Growth

What matters: TikTok Ads Manager, short-form video, Canva, content that converts.

How to write it:
“Created and A/B tested ad creatives for TikTok campaigns promoting local events, reaching ~15,000 views in 7 days.”

That sounds like agency work — it's not “I like Instagram.”

Finance / Business / Ops

What matters: Excel (PivotTables, VLOOKUP), PowerPoint, budget tracking.

How to write it:
“Built monthly cashflow and expense tracking spreadsheets in Excel for a student organization and presented insights to leadership.”

You sound like you’re already doing analyst work.

Data / Tech

What matters: Python automation, basic SQL, GitHub, data cleaning.

How to write it:
“Used Python to collect and clean 500+ product listings from public sites and stored the results in a searchable Excel sheet.”

This shows you build things, not just study them.

Add these kinds of bullets to your “Skills” and “Experience” sections. That’s what gets you interviews.
