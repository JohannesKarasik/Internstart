Understanding the Finance Industry Landscape

Finance spans banking, markets, and corporate decision-making. Knowing the lanes helps you aim your learning and outreach.

The major sectors

  • Banking: retail, commercial, and investment
  • Asset & Wealth Management
  • Corporate Finance & FP&A
  • Accounting & Audit
  • Financial Technology (FinTech)

Typical entry roles

Financial Analyst, Investment Banking Analyst, Junior Accountant, Equity Research Associate, Risk Analyst, Credit Analyst. Senior tracks include Portfolio Manager, Controller, VP/Director, and CFO.

Step 1 — Start with the Right Education

Best degrees

Finance, Economics, Accounting, Business Administration, Mathematics/Statistics. Non-traditional majors can work—prove quantitative reasoning and business judgment.

Alternative pathways

Use MOOCs (Coursera, edX, Udemy) to learn financial modeling, valuation, Excel, Python. Ship course capstones as portfolio artifacts (models, dashboards).

Step 2 — Develop Essential Finance Skills

Analytical & quantitative

  • Excel (INDEX-MATCH/XLOOKUP, PivotTables), 3-statement & DCF modeling
  • Accounting fluency (IS, BS, CF), ratios, variance analysis

Communication & stakeholder skills

Turn complex numbers into simple narratives for leadership and clients.

Modern tech stack

Python/SQL for analysis and automation; use AI-assisted research to speed insight.

Step 3 — Gain Real-World Experience Early

Internships & part-time roles

Start with boutique firms, local banks, or corporate FP&A to learn live processes (close, forecasting, decks).

Step 4 — Build a Strong Professional Network

Optimize LinkedIn, post one weekly market insight, and message alumni with precise asks (e.g., “15 minutes on recruiting timelines”). Track touches in a simple CRM sheet.

Step 5 — Earn Professional Certifications

CFA, CPA, FRM (plus CIMA/ACCA regionally) boost credibility. Even CFA Level I signals commitment and baseline competence.

Step 6 — Create a Finance-Focused Resume & Portfolio

Quantify everything

“Built a 3-statement model for a $12M SaaS; informed pricing—projected +6% margin.” Numbers beat adjectives.

Step 7 — Prepare for Finance Interviews

  • Walk a DCF and sensitivity tables
  • Explain working capital and cash conversion
  • Discuss a recent deal/market move and your view

Bring proof: a 1-page equity pitch or KPI dashboard sparks better conversations than buzzwords.

Step 9 — Stay Updated on Financial Trends

Follow reputable outlets daily; track rates, inflation, and jobs data. Keep a weekly macro diary with three takeaways.

Step 10 — Keep Growing via Internships, Mentorship & Learning

Run monthly skill sprints (Power Query, pandas, deck design). Pair with a mentor or study group for accountability.

FAQ

Do I need a finance degree?

No—show competency via projects, certifications, and clear communication.

How do I start with no experience?

Build a model, a stock pitch, or a KPI dashboard; then use Internstart to target internships and send tailored applications.

Which certifications are best?

CFA (markets/IB/AM), CPA (accounting/corp), FRM (risk). Even partial progress helps.

How should I prep for interviews?

Practice DCF, accounting linkages, and a recent deal/market view; bring a one-pager project.
