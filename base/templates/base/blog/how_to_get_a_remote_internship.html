What a remote internship is (and isn’t)

A remote internship is a structured learning role with goals, deadlines, and a manager—delivered online via tools like Slack, Zoom, Docs, Git, and Figma. It’s not a loose volunteer gig. Look for clear scope, a point of contact, and outcomes you can add to your resume.

How to get a remote internship in 5 steps

StepActionWhy it matters
1) Choose a lane Pick one track (marketing • software • data • design • ops/finance). Clarifies your keywords, projects, and company list.
2) Build tiny proof Ship a 1–2 day project (repo, dashboard, content calendar, mock redesign). Replaces “experience” with a linkable result.
3) Apply early & broadly Submit in the first week; target 50–150 relevant roles over the season. Remote postings are rolling; early beats perfect.
4) Optimize your top third Put proof links and exact keywords at the top of your resume. Passes ATS and convinces humans fast.
5) Demonstrate async skills Provide concise written updates; link docs; propose overlap hours. Signals you’ll succeed in distributed teams.

Application strategy & ATS tips

  • Mirror exact phrases: Lift keywords directly from the posting (e.g., “PivotTables,” “React,” “customer interviews,” “Figma prototypes”).
  • Top-third proof: Link a small project at the very top—repo, dashboard, or portfolio page.
  • Short cover letter: 2-line hook → 3–5 keyword fit → 1 result → availability. See cover letter for internship.
  • Name files clearly: Firstname-Lastname-Remote-Internship-Resume.pdf.
  • Submit early & track: First week after posting + simple tracker (company, link, date, status).

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Generic applications: No keywords, no proof link, sent late.
  • Invisible progress: Not documenting work in async teams.
  • Time-zone surprises: Not proposing overlap or SLAs up front.
  • All talk, no link: Always attach a small project, repo, or doc.

FAQ

How many applications should I send for remote roles?

Aim for 50–150 targeted submissions across the season. Remote roles are competitive; steady volume wins.

What should my resume show?

Evidence of async success: clear writing, linked docs/dashboards/repos, and shipped outcomes with numbers.

What if I’ve never worked remotely?

Run a tiny project in public (GitHub, Figma, Looker, Notion). Document decisions and results. Link it in the top third of your resume.
