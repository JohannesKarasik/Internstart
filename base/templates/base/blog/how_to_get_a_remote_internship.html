What a remote internship is

A remote internship is a real role with goals, feedback, and a manager—just done online. You’ll meet on video, chat in Slack or Teams, and share work in Docs, GitHub, or Figma. Look for a clear scope and someone responsible for your guidance.

How to get a remote internship in five steps

StepDo thisWhy it helps
1) Pick a lane Choose one area: marketing, software, data, design, or operations. Makes your resume focused and your search faster.
2) Make a tiny project Build something in 1–2 days: a mini dashboard, small app, content samples, or a simple redesign. Gives you a link that proves you can do the work.
3) Apply early and often Submit within the first week of posting; aim for steady weekly applications. Remote roles are global—early birds get seen.
4) Put proof at the top Add your best link and 3–5 matching keywords in the top third of your resume. Helps both the screening system and the hiring manager.
5) Show you can work async Share short written updates and keep files tidy and easy to open. Managers trust people who communicate clearly.

Best places to look for remote internships

PlaceHow to use itWhy it’s good
Company career pages Make a list of 50 favorites and check weekly. Listings often appear here first.
University portals Filter by “remote” and your tools (Excel, Python, Figma). Many employers expect limited experience.
Startups & small agencies Search for “remote intern” and “student.” Decisions are faster; initiative matters.
Nonprofits Offer a clear project (content plan, CRM cleanup, simple dashboard). Flexible schedules and strong references.
LinkedIn Set job alerts and comment on hiring posts to be visible. Connections reveal roles that aren’t on boards yet.

How to stand out when you apply

  • Match the words they use. Copy key phrases from the posting (for example “React,” “Figma prototypes,” or “customer research”).
  • Lead with a link. Put your demo, repo, or portfolio link at the top of your resume.
  • Keep your cover letter short. Hook → 3–5 skills they want → one quick result → your availability. See our cover letter for internship guide.
  • Name files clearly. Firstname-Lastname-Remote-Internship-Resume.pdf.
  • Track it. Keep a simple sheet with company, link, date, and status.

Common mistakes

  • Sending generic applications. No keywords, no link, sent late.
  • Not showing progress. In remote teams, written updates matter.
  • Ignoring time zones. Don’t wait—suggest overlap hours in your first message.
  • Only talking about goals. Share a link to something you’ve made.

FAQ

How many applications should I send?

Think steady and targeted. Aim for regular weekly applications during the season rather than one big burst.

What should my resume show?

Clear writing, a link to real work, and a few skills the posting repeats. One page is perfect.

I’ve never worked remotely—how do I prove I can do it?

Create a tiny project in public (GitHub, Figma, Looker, Notion). Write one page that explains the goal, what you did, and the result. Link it at the top of your resume.
